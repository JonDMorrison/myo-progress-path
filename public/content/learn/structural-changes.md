# Structural Changes Caused by Myofunctional Disorders

## Oral

- High narrow palate that limits nasal cavity space and prevents nasal breathing
- Orthodontic issues (overbite, underbite, open bite, cross bite, crowding or spacing)
  - Palatal expander or sagittal appliance is often recommended for children
  - For adults whose palate has fused, surgical intervention may be recommended (SARPE or MARPE)
  - Without myofunctional therapy after orthodontics, orthodontic relapse is common
- Increase in inflammation
- Erosion of teeth due to acid reflux
- Heightened gag reflex
- Clenching or grinding
- Scalloped tongue
- Gummy smile
- Very pronounced palatal rugae
- Tongue thrust

## Facial

- Long, narrow face
- Dark circles under the eyes
- Under developed jaw
- Poorly developing cheekbones
- Low facial muscle tone
- Dimpled chin
- Bump on bridge of nose
