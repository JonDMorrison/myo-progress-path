# Structural Changes Caused by Myofunctional Disorders

Myofunctional disorders can cause significant structural changes to the face, mouth, and airway over time. Understanding these changes helps explain why myofunctional therapy is important.

## Oral Structural Changes

### High, Narrow Palate

When the tongue doesn't rest on the roof of the mouth, significant structural changes occur:

![Normal vs high-arched palate comparison](/images/learn/palate-comparison.jpg)

- High and narrow (vaulted)
- Constricted, reducing nasal breathing space
- More likely to cause crowded teeth

**Impact:** A high, narrow palate reduces space for the tongue and can obstruct nasal breathing.

### Dental Crowding and Misalignment

Improper tongue position and mouth breathing can lead to orthodontic problems:

- Crowded, crooked teeth
- Open bite or overbite
- Crossbite
- Need for orthodontic treatment

**Impact:** Without proper tongue support, the teeth and jaws may not develop correctly.

### TMJ Issues

Improper muscle patterns can cause painful jaw dysfunction:

- Jaw pain and clicking
- Headaches
- Limited jaw movement
- Grinding or clenching

**Impact:** Compensatory muscle use puts stress on the temporomandibular joint.

## Facial Structural Changes

### Long Face Syndrome

Chronic mouth breathing can lead to characteristic facial changes:

- Elongated facial structure
- Recessed chin
- Gummy smile
- Narrow mid-face

**Impact:** The face develops vertically rather than forward, affecting aesthetics and function.

### Reduced Airway Space

When facial development is affected, the airway suffers:

- Narrower and more collapsible
- More prone to obstruction during sleep
- Less efficient for breathing

**Impact:** Smaller airways increase risk of sleep-disordered breathing and sleep apnea.

### Flattened Cheekbones

Without proper tongue support and nasal breathing, facial aesthetics change:

- Cheekbones may not develop fully
- Mid-face appears flatter
- Under-eye hollows may develop

**Impact:** Affects facial aesthetics and can reduce nasal breathing capacity.

## Postural Changes

### Forward Head Posture

To maintain an open airway with mouth breathing, the body compensates:

- Head tilts forward
- Neck and shoulders strain
- Spine alignment affected

**Impact:** Can lead to chronic pain and postural issues throughout the body.

## The Good News

While some structural changes are permanent once growth is complete, myofunctional therapy can:

- Prevent further deterioration
- Improve muscle function even with existing structural issues
- Support orthodontic and surgical treatments
- Optimize whatever structure is present
- In children, guide proper development before it's too late

Early intervention in children can prevent many of these structural changes from occurring in the first place.
