# The Four Goals of Myofunctional Therapy

Myofunctional therapy focuses on achieving four primary goals that work together to improve oral and facial muscle function. These goals form the foundation of the entire program.

## Nasal Breathing

**Why it matters:** Breathing through the nose is essential for proper oxygenation, filtration of air, and production of nitric oxide, which improves oxygen absorption.

**Key benefits:**
- Filters and humidifies incoming air
- Produces nitric oxide for better oxygen delivery
- Supports proper facial development
- Reduces risk of sleep-disordered breathing
- Improves overall health and immunity

**What you'll learn:**
- How to maintain nasal breathing during the day
- Techniques to keep nasal passages clear
- Exercises to strengthen nasal breathing patterns

## Lip Seal

**Why it matters:** Maintaining a gentle lip seal at rest ensures nasal breathing and prevents the tongue from falling back in the airway.

**Key benefits:**
- Supports nasal breathing
- Prevents dry mouth
- Reduces risk of dental issues
- Improves facial aesthetics
- Supports proper jaw development

**What you'll learn:**
- How to maintain a relaxed lip seal without tension
- Exercises to strengthen lip muscles
- Awareness of lip position throughout the day

## Chewing and Swallowing

**Why it matters:** Proper chewing and swallowing patterns prevent compensatory muscle use and support healthy facial development.

**Key benefits:**
- Reduces TMJ stress
- Improves digestion
- Supports proper facial muscle development
- Prevents dental issues
- Enhances facial symmetry

**What you'll learn:**
- Correct chewing patterns using both sides
- Proper swallowing without using facial muscles
- Awareness of tongue position during swallowing

## Tongue Posture

**Why it matters:** The tongue should rest on the roof of the mouth at the "spot" (incisive papilla) behind the upper front teeth. This position supports the palate, maintains the airway, and supports proper facial growth.

**Key benefits:**
- Supports the palate and maxilla
- Keeps the airway open
- Improves facial development
- Reduces TMJ issues
- Improves speech clarity

**What you'll learn:**
- How to find the "spot" (incisive papilla)
- Exercises to strengthen tongue position
- Maintaining tongue posture all day

![Photo B: The "Spot"](/images/learn/the-spot.jpg)

## How These Goals Work Together

All four goals are interconnected. Nasal breathing requires a lip seal. A lip seal supports proper tongue posture. Proper tongue posture improves swallowing and chewing. When all four work together, the result is improved breathing, better sleep, enhanced facial development, and overall better health.
