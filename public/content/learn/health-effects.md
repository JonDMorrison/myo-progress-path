# Health/Systemic Effects Associated with Myofunctional Disorders

- Activation of sympathetic "fight or flight" nervous system due to airway/oxygen stress
  - Can increase anxiety
  - Can lead to clenching
  - Limits body's ability to enter parasympathetic "rest and digest" nervous system
- Digestive Issues
  - Acid Reflux/GERD
  - IBS
  - Heartburn
  - Bloating
  - Difficulty chewing
  - Laryngopharyngeal Reflux: Constant throat clearing, postnasal drip, feeling a lump in the throat
- Lowered immune function
  - Nasal breathing allows for filtration of air
- Heart Disease
  - Poor Circulation
  - High Blood Pressure
  - Heart Attack and Stroke
- ADHD, aggression and poor emotional regulation in kids
  - Poor sleep quality and decreased oxygen to the brain
- Speech Difficulties
  - S Sound, R Sound, L Sound or Lisp
  - Lack of Clarity or Mumbling
  - Limited Voice Projection
- Migraines
  - Altered oxygen levels to the brain
- TMD
  - Forward jaw position to help open airway
  - From poor skeletal growth
  - Muscle bracing due to weak muscle coordination/tone
  - If present with a tongue tie, the tongue cannot achieve proper posture due to limited mobility so teeth clench to help tongue reach correct position
- Shoulder pain and tension
  - Forward head posture to help open airway
  - Tongue tie can restrict fascia from head to toe
- History of multiple ear infections, especially as a child
  - Proper swallowing helps drain Eustacian tubes in ears
  - Vertigo or Tinnitus related to inner ear issues
- Alzheimer's and Dementia
- Hyper or Hypo Thyroidism
- Full body muscle/fascial tightness
  - Associated with tongue tie
- Sleep Apnea
