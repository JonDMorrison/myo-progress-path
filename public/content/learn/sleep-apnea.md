# How Myofunctional Therapy Improves Sleep Apnea

Myofunctional Therapy is a great tool for helping to manage obstructive sleep apnea. Therapy helps by strengthening the tongue muscles, allowing the tongue to maintain proper posture during sleep and prevent the obstruction of the airway. Therapy can also make CPAP wear more comfortable for those who still require it after therapy has been completed. Therapy can reduce the airflow needed from the CPAP by limiting obstructions or allowing different mask options. Unfortunately, without intervention sleep apnea will progress as the lack of oxygen damages the brain over time and decreases its ability to alert or wake you during sleep. What starts out as snoring can progress to Upper Airway Resistance Syndrome (a very light sleep with frequent waking due to airway stress) and/or to Sleep Apnea (mild to severe sleep apnea ranges from 5 to 30+ apneic episodes per hour). People who suffer from any of the myofunctional disorders have a higher likelihood of developing sleep apnea and increasing their risk of the associated health complications, including those listed above.
