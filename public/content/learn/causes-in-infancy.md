# Causes of Myofunctional Disorders During Infancy

There are many contributing factors to myofunctional disorders starting during infancy. A C-section and prolonged or difficult labour can alter facial bones which may affect feeding and breathing habits. Feeding habits are also impacted for babies born preterm as they will not have fully developed buccal fat pads at the time of birth. Infants learn proper oral muscle coordination during feeding and the type of feeding has an impact on this. Breast feeding requires more facial muscle activation and less tongue muscle activation than bottle feeding. For babies born with a tongue tie, this causes restricted movement with their tongue which affects feedings, tongue posture, mobility, strength and will result in problematic compensations from other muscles. Toddlers can also develop habits that contribute to myofunctional disorders. Thumb, finger, and blanket/clothes sucking can have an effect on muscle function. The prolonged use of pacifiers and sippy cups have also been shown to have this effect.
