# Signs of Sleep-Disordered Breathing in Children

Children with sleep-disordered breathing often show different symptoms than adults. Recognizing these signs early can lead to intervention that prevents long-term health and developmental issues.

## Sleep-Related Signs

### Nighttime Symptoms

- **Snoring** - Frequent or loud snoring is never normal in children
- **Mouth breathing during sleep** - Should breathe through nose
- **Restless sleep** - Tossing, turning, frequent position changes
- **Night sweats** - Soaking through pajamas or sheets
- **Sleeping in unusual positions** - Head hanging off bed, hyperextended neck
- **Bedwetting** past age 5-7
- **Night terrors or nightmares**
- **Sleep talking or sleepwalking**

### Morning Symptoms

- Difficulty waking up
- Morning headaches
- Dry mouth or sore throat
- Congested or stuffy nose

## Daytime Signs

### Behavioral Issues

- **Hyperactivity** - Often misdiagnosed as ADHD
- **Impulsivity and difficulty focusing**
- **Irritability and mood swings**
- **Aggression or defiance**
- **Social difficulties**

Many children with sleep-disordered breathing are labeled as having behavioral problems when the root cause is poor sleep quality.

### Physical Symptoms

- **Chronic fatigue or appearing tired**
- **Falling asleep at inappropriate times** (in car, during quiet activities)
- **Poor appetite**
- **Slow growth or failure to thrive**
- **Frequent illnesses**

### Cognitive and Academic Signs

- Difficulty concentrating
- Memory problems
- Learning difficulties
- Declining grades
- Difficulty completing tasks

## Physical Appearance

### Facial Features

- **Long, narrow face** (adenoid facies)
- **Open mouth posture** at rest
- **Dark circles under eyes**
- **Flattened cheekbones**
- **Recessed chin**
- **Gummy smile**
- **Crowded or crooked teeth**

### Oral Features

- **High, narrow palate**
- **Large or inflamed tonsils and adenoids**
- **Tongue tie or lip tie**
- **Dental crowding**
- **Crossbite or open bite**

### Postural Signs

- Forward head posture
- Slouched shoulders
- Mouth hanging open

## Breathing Patterns

- **Mouth breathing** during the day
- **Rapid, shallow breathing**
- **Noisy breathing**
- **Gasping or choking during sleep**
- **Pauses in breathing** during sleep (witnessed apneas)

## Medical Conditions Often Associated

- Chronic allergies
- Frequent ear infections
- Chronic sinusitis
- Enlarged tonsils and adenoids
- Asthma
- Eczema

## The Impact on Development

Sleep-disordered breathing in children can affect:

### Brain Development
- Reduced oxygen to developing brain
- Impacts learning, memory, and behavior
- Can affect IQ and academic performance

### Physical Growth
- Growth hormone is released during deep sleep
- Poor sleep can slow physical development
- May affect height and weight

### Facial Development
- Mouth breathing changes facial growth patterns
- Can lead to permanent structural changes
- Early intervention can guide proper development

## When to Seek Help

Consult a healthcare provider if your child has:

- **Any witnessed pauses in breathing during sleep**
- **Loud or frequent snoring**
- **Behavioral issues** that don't improve with standard interventions
- **Chronic mouth breathing**
- **Poor school performance** despite adequate intelligence
- **Multiple signs** from the lists above

## Importance of Early Intervention

**Children's faces are still growing**, which means:

- Myofunctional therapy can guide proper development
- Structural changes can be prevented
- Earlier intervention = better outcomes
- Habits are easier to change when young

Early treatment can prevent a lifetime of health issues, from dental problems to sleep apnea as an adult.

## What to Do

If you notice several of these signs:

1. **Document symptoms** - Keep a log of snoring, behavior, sleep quality
2. **Talk to your dentist or pediatrician** - They can evaluate for sleep-disordered breathing
3. **Consider a sleep study** - Especially if symptoms are severe
4. **Evaluate for tongue tie, tonsils, allergies** - Address anatomical issues
5. **Start myofunctional therapy** - Even young children can benefit from age-appropriate exercises

## The Good News

Children respond quickly to intervention. With proper treatment:

- Sleep quality improves rapidly
- Behavior often normalizes
- Academic performance increases
- Facial development can be guided properly
- Long-term health risks are reduced

The earlier intervention happens, the better the outcomes for your child's health, development, and quality of life.
