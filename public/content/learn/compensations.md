# Compensations to Limit

In this context, compensations are the result of stronger neck, face and oral muscles taking over for muscles that are underdeveloped. In order to retrain these underdeveloped muscles, it is very important to prevent this from occurring. Compensations will need to be evaluated and controlled **before your tongue tie release is approved** (Program dependant).

## Neck Engagement

**What it looks like:** The muscles of the neck visibly contract or strain during oral exercises, especially during facial movements such as smiling.

**Why it happens:** Neck muscles compensate for weak facial muscles, attempting to assist with movements the face should perform independently.

## Jaw Protrusion

<div style="position:relative;padding-bottom:56.25%;height:0;overflow:hidden;max-width:100%;"><iframe src="https://player.vimeo.com/video/1143169821?badge=0&autopause=0&player_id=0&app_id=58479" frameborder="0" allow="autoplay; fullscreen; picture-in-picture; clipboard-write; encrypted-media" style="position:absolute;top:0;left:0;width:100%;height:100%;" title="Jaw Protrusion"></iframe></div>

**What it looks like:** The jaw shifts forward noticeably during exercises.

**Why it happens:** The jaw moves forward to compensate for weak posterior (back) tongue muscles that should be doing the work.

## Jaw Lateralization

<div style="position:relative;padding-bottom:56.25%;height:0;overflow:hidden;max-width:100%;"><iframe src="https://player.vimeo.com/video/1143169739?badge=0&autopause=0&player_id=0&app_id=58479" frameborder="0" allow="autoplay; fullscreen; picture-in-picture; clipboard-write; encrypted-media" style="position:absolute;top:0;left:0;width:100%;height:100%;" title="Jaw Lateralization"></iframe></div>

**What it looks like:** The jaw shifts side to side during exercises or opening the jaw.

**Why it happens:** Like protrusion, this compensates for weak posterior tongue muscles or indicates TMJ concerns.

## Floor of Mouth Activation

![Floor of Mouth Activation](/images/learn/compensations/floor-of-mouth.jpg)

**What it looks like:** The floor of the mouth (the area under the tongue and behind the lower front teeth) lifts during tongue exercises.

**Why it happens:** The floor of the mouth lifts to compensate for weak tongue muscles or restricted movement from a tongue tie.

## Facial Grimace

**What it looks like:** Lips rounding or pursing, cheeks pulling inward, and/or the chin dimples or shows strain—often during opening the mouth and swallowing.

![Facial Grimace](/images/learn/compensations/facial-grimace.jpg)

**Why it happens:** Facial muscles overwork to compensate for weak posterior tongue muscles, tension, and poor muscle coordination.

## Tension

**What it looks like:** Tension held through any muscles of the body that are not needed for the exercise. This could be shoulders, neck, facial muscles etc.

**Why it happens:** This is a common habit when trying to build muscle tone and coordination. It is important to keep the rest of your body relaxed while focusing on your exercises to prevent other compensations from occurring.

## Mouth Breathing

**What it looks like:** Breathing through the mouth instead of the nose at any time.

**Why it happens:** This may happen due to habit, poor muscle tone, or nasal congestion. It is important to prioritize nasal breathing. The more you breathe through your nose, the less congested you will be. Mouth breathing causes over breathing which leads to the body causing more congestion. If you are feeling congested, please use the Nasal Unblocking Exercises provided. These exercises will be very helpful for the duration of your program, it is important to use as needed to encourage nasal breathing at all times, even when sick or suffering from allergies.
