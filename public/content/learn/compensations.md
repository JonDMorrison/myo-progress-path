# Compensations to Limit (Include Photos)

In this context, **compensations** are the result of stronger neck, face and oral muscles taking over for muscles that are underdeveloped. To retrain these underdeveloped muscles, it is very important to prevent compensations. Compensations will need to be evaluated and controlled **before your tongue tie release is approved**.

## Neck Engagement

*Muscles of the neck compensating for weak facial muscles*

## Jaw Protrusion

*Jaw shifting forward to compensate for weak posterior tongue muscles*

## Jaw Lateralization

*Jaw shifting side to side to compensate for weak posterior tongue muscles*

## Floor of Mouth Activation

*Floor of the mouth lifting to compensate for weak posterior tongue muscles or tongue tie*

## Facial Grimace

*Rounding/pursing lips, cheeks pulling in, and/or chin dimpling or strain—often during opening or swallowing*

## Tension

*Holding tension throughout any muscles of the body due to weak muscle coordination/tone; this can trigger compensations*

## Mouth Breathing - Nasal Unblocking

If a plugged nose is preventing nasal breathing, use these:

### Exercise 1 — Sinus Massage

- Rub along the **sides of nose**, **cheeks** beside the nose, and **above eyebrows**.
- If your nose gets runny, **blow** (don't sniffle).
- Continue until congestion eases.

### Exercise 2 — Breath-Hold Reset

1. Take **3 normal breaths**, in/out naturally.
2. After the 3rd exhale, **pinch your nose** and hold your breath.
3. Gently **nod** head up/down or side/side.
4. Hold until you feel a **mild air hunger**.
5. **Mouth closed**, inhale **through your nose**; keep nose breathing as it normalizes.
6. Repeat **3–5 times** as needed.

**Tips**

- If only one nostril is blocked, **cover the clear side** and breathe through the blocked nostril to open it.
- Don't talk/laugh or mouth-breathe during the drill—it slows results.
- If no air passes initially, do Exercise 1 first or a small amount of nasal spray.

> The more you breathe through your nose, the **less congested** you'll be.
